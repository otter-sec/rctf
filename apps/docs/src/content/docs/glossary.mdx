---
title: Glossary
description: Definitions for common CTF terms used across challenges, scoring, teams, and leaderboards.
---

This page defines common Capture the Flag terms. It keeps the scope to words participants and organizers usually use during an event.

## C

### Challenge

A task that participants solve to earn points. Challenges usually include a prompt, one or more files or services, and a flag that proves the team found the intended answer.

### Category

A label that groups similar challenges. Common categories include web, crypto, pwn, reverse engineering, forensics, and miscellaneous.

### CTF

Short for Capture the Flag. In this style of security competition, teams solve challenges and submit flags for points.

## D

### Division

A separate leaderboard group inside the same event. Divisions are often used for eligibility groups such as open, student, high school, onsite, or invited teams.

### Dynamic scoring

A scoring system where a challenge can become worth fewer points as more teams solve it. This helps early solves on difficult challenges matter more than later solves on widely solved challenges.

## F

### First blood

The first accepted solve on a challenge. Some events also recognize second blood and third blood for the next two accepted solves.

### Flag

The answer submitted for a challenge. Flags are usually formatted as a recognizable string so the scoring system can check whether a submission is correct.

## H

### Hint

Extra information released to help teams make progress on a challenge. Some events make hints free, while others attach a point penalty or release them after a delay.

## L

### Last solve time

The time of a team's most recent accepted solve. Many CTFs use this as a leaderboard tiebreaker, where the team that reached the same score earlier ranks higher.

### Leaderboard

The ranking of teams during or after the event. Leaderboards usually sort by score first and then use one or more tiebreakers.

## P

### Points

The score awarded for solving a challenge. Point values may be fixed, dynamic, or adjusted manually by organizers.

## S

### Sanity check

A simple challenge that helps teams confirm they can log in, submit flags, and understand the event format.

### Solve

An accepted flag submission for a challenge. A solve usually awards points and records the time when the team completed the challenge.

### Submission

An attempt to submit a flag. Submissions can be accepted, rejected, or ignored depending on the event rules and challenge state.

## T

### Team

One or more participants competing together under the same score.

### Tiebreaker

A rule used when two teams have the same score. Common tiebreakers include last solve time, earliest final score, or the time of the most recent solve on selected challenges.

### Tiebreak eligible solve

A solve that counts toward the leaderboard tiebreaker. Events may exclude warmup, survey, sanity check, or post event challenges from tiebreaking.
